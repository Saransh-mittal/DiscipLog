## 1. The Problem

Modern productivity tools are **passive ledgers**. They wait for you to open them. They store data. They show you a graph. Then they close. The pattern repeats indefinitely without any meaningful change in behavior.

Here's what existing tools consistently get wrong:

| Problem | What apps do today |
|---|---|
| **No memory** | Log it today, forget it tomorrow. No recall, no spaced repetition. |
| **No voice** | Typing interrupts flow. Nobody wants to fill out a form mid-session. |
| **No intelligence** | Data collected, never acted on. The user is always their own analyst. |
| **No accountability** | Too easy to skip. No consequence for breaking a habit. |
| **No atmosphere** | Every day looks identical — no visual feedback for consistency. |
| **No coaching** | No system that actually talks back and helps you improve. |

The result? Apps that get abandoned within two weeks because they deliver no real value beyond storage.

---

## 2. What DiscipLog Is

**DiscipLog is an AI-powered discipline tracking system** that transforms raw focus sessions into a living, evolving record of your growth — complete with:

- **Voice-first logging** — describe what you did, AI summarizes it in seconds
- **Sprint timer** — timed focus blocks with structured post-session check-ins
- **AI Coach** — a conversational personal strategist who actually reads your data
- **Smart Recall** — a spaced repetition system built from your own sessions
- **Momentum / World Tiers** — a visual layer that evolves as your streak grows
- **Weekly Cinematic Debriefs** — AI-narrated performance reviews every Sunday
- **Proactive Push Nudges** — the app messages you at your optimal logging time
- **Commitment Tracker** — weekly goals with AI-enhanced accountability

DiscipLog doesn't just watch you work. **It coaches, recalls, challenges, and rewards you.**

---

## 3. Who It's For

DiscipLog is for anyone with **multiple active disciplines** they want to grow deliberately:

- Developers shipping side projects alongside a day job
- Musicians building a daily practice
- Writers maintaining a creative routine
- Students managing multi-subject study schedules
- Founders splitting time between deep work and operations
- Athletes tracking training and recovery
- Researchers logging experiments and reading hours

The key insight: **this is not a habit tracker, not a to-do list, not a journal.** It's a *performance system* — closer to what a professional coach would design for you than anything that currently exists in consumer software.

## 4. The Full User Journey

### 4.1 First Contact — Landing Page

The user arrives at a near-black, cinematic landing screen. A pulsing badge announces **"AI-Powered Discipline"**. The headline — *DiscipLog* — sits in a bold display typeface with a single call to action: **"Enter Dashboard"**.

No feature lists. No pricing. No testimonials. Just a challenge to show up.

**Design intention:** Signal premium quality and seriousness. The app isn't trying to sell itself — it's daring you to commit.

### 4.2 AI Onboarding — Setting Up Your World

New users are guided through a **4-step AI-powered setup flow** before they reach the dashboard.

#### Step 1 — Describe Your Life in Plain Language

A large text area with an animated microphone button. The user speaks or types something like:

> *"I study system design, build a side project, go to the gym, and try to read every evening."*

They press **Generate Categories** and the AI analyzes their description to produce a personalized tracking plan.

#### Step 2 — Review and Adjust Categories

The AI response populates an editable list of categories. For the example above it might produce:

| Category | Daily Target | Weekly Range | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| System Design | 2 hrs | 10–14 hrs | Main Goal |
| Side Project | 1.5 hrs | 7–10 hrs | Main Goal |
| Gym | 1 hr | 5–7 hrs | Main Goal |
| Reading | 30 min | 2–4 hrs | Side Quest |

Each card shows an AI-suggested icon, the category name (editable), daily and weekly targets, and whether it's a **Main Goal** or **Side Quest**. Users can add, remove, or adjust everything. Maximum of 7 categories.

#### Step 3 — Choose Your AI Coach Persona

Four coaching archetypes:

| Persona | Style |
|---|---|
| **The Drill Sergeant** | Tough love, zero excuses, aggressive accountability |
| **The Mentor** | Empathetic, steady, emotionally intelligent |
| **The Analyst** | Data-first, diagnostic, optimization-focused |
| **The Hype Man** | High-energy, momentum-building, relentlessly positive |

The user also writes their **Core Why** (the deeper motivation driving their discipline) and optional **Custom Instructions** — anything specific they want the coach to always remember.

#### Step 4 — World Preview and Launch

A brief preview of the 5 world tiers they'll unlock as their streak grows, followed by entry into the main dashboard.

**Why this matters:** The user starts with a system built around *their* life, not a blank slate. The AI does in 2 minutes what would otherwise take hours of manual setup. This is the single highest-leverage moment in the app — it converts a generic tracker into *their* tracker.

### 4.3 The Dashboard — Overview

The main dashboard is the mission control center. It has five navigation tabs: **Overview · Log · History · Settings · Archive** (Archive appears only when past weekly debriefs exist).

The **Overview** tab shows:

- **Daily Progress bars** — one per category, filling in real time as sessions are logged against daily targets
- **Weekly Progress bars** — accumulated hours across the current Monday-to-Sunday week, with minimum and maximum range markers
- **12-week Activity Heatmap** — a grid of day squares shaded in amber gradients based on how much was logged. Dark = light day. Bright gold = peak effort.
- **Momentum Flame** — a living flame icon that scales in intensity from cold grey to blazing gold, reflecting both today's output and the current streak
- **Weekly Commitments widget** — the active week's declared goals with checkboxes
- **Smart Recall Bonus Card** — shows whether there are recall cards ready to review, scheduled for later, or still locked
- **End-of-Day Micro Review** — a lightweight evening reflection prompt that appears when it's late in the day and the user has been active

The entire visual character of the app — colors, card surfaces, animations — shifts based on the user's current streak tier.

**Why this matters:** The overview answers "where do I stand today?" in under 3 seconds. No data interpretation required. It's a command center, not a spreadsheet.

### 4.4 Logging a Session

The **Log** tab offers two ways to capture a session.

#### Manual Log

1. Select a category from the configured list
2. Set the duration (e.g. 1.5 hours)
3. Speak or type what was done — a transcript area with a live microphone button
4. Click **Save** — the AI immediately reads the transcript and produces a clean 3-bullet summary
5. All dashboard widgets, the heatmap, and the Smart Recall queue update instantly

**Voice flow:** Clicking the mic activates speech recognition. A waveform animation pulses while listening. The transcript fills in naturally as the user speaks. No clean-up needed — the AI handles it.

**Why this matters:** Logging takes under 60 seconds and produces a polished, AI-written record. The barrier is low enough that it becomes a genuine daily ritual.

### 4.5 Sprint Timer — Focused Work Blocks

The Sprint Timer is a focus module that turns DiscipLog from a logger into an active work companion.

#### Setting Up a Sprint

- Choose a duration: preset chips for **25 min · 50 min · 90 min**, or type any value from 1 to 240 minutes
- Select the category
- Hit **Start**

#### During the Sprint

- A large countdown display dominates the card
- **Pause / Resume** controls for interruptions
- **Finish Now** button to end early and move to check-in
- The browser tab title shows the live countdown — so the timer is visible even when working in another tab
- If the browser is accidentally closed, the sprint state is preserved and restores automatically on next open

#### Post-Sprint Check-In

When time runs out (or Finish Now is pressed):
1. A three-note completion chime plays
2. A dialogue opens asking: *what did you actually work on?*
3. The user types or dictates their notes with the mic button
4. Clicking **Save Sprint** summarizes the notes with AI and stores the session — including how long was planned versus how long was actually worked

**Why this matters:** The timer makes the app useful *during* work, not just after. The automatic state recovery means a crash or tab close never loses a session. The post-sprint check-in builds the reflection habit naturally, as part of the flow rather than as an extra step.

### 4.6 History Feed — Reviewing the Past

The **History** tab is the complete record of every session ever logged — a chronological ledger the user can read, correct, and search through.

#### Three Views

| View | What it shows |
|---|---|
| All Sessions | Full history, newest first |
| This Week | Monday-to-Sunday filter |
| Today | Current day only |

#### What Each Entry Shows

Every log card displays the category, duration, timestamp, how the session was captured (manual or sprint), and the AI-generated summary rendered as clean styled bullet points.

#### Editing a Log

Clicking **Edit** opens a dedicated editor panel. The user can:
- Change the category
- Adjust the duration
- Edit the timestamp — and when they do, the entire system updates: the heatmap cell, progress bars, and streak calculation all reflect the corrected date immediately
- Rewrite the transcript
- Click **Regenerate Summary** to get a fresh AI summary from the updated transcript before saving

#### Deleting a Log

A confirmation step prevents accidental deletion. Once confirmed, the log is permanently removed and all widgets recalculate.

**Why this matters:** The history feed is where the user audits their own discipline. The ability to correct timestamps means a log from last night that accidentally landed on the wrong day can be fixed without data integrity issues cascading through the system.

### 4.7 AI Coach — Your Personal Strategist

The AI Coach is accessible from a **floating button fixed to the bottom-right corner** of every page. It's always one click away.

#### Opening the Drawer

Clicking it slides open a full-height panel from the right side. The panel stays alive as the user navigates between tabs — the conversation never resets mid-session.

#### Automatic Daily Insight

The first time the coach opens each session, it sends a greeting automatically — a brief, data-driven read on today's progress and the week so far. The user doesn't have to ask anything to get value.

#### What the Coach Knows

Every reply the coach sends is informed by:

- All recent log entries and the transcripts inside them
- This week's targets versus what's actually been logged, per category
- The active weekly commitments and their current statuses
- The user's coach persona, personal motivation (Core Why), and custom instructions
- **Implicit Memory** — an automatically maintained behavioral profile the AI builds up over time, updated after every session and every conversation. It captures patterns the user may not notice consciously: *tends to log late at night*, *consistency dips on Wednesdays*, *deep work sessions are shorter when gym is also logged*.

#### Deep Research

When the coach needs to go deeper — for example if asked about a specific past period or a pattern across months — it can search through the entire log history to find relevant sessions before answering. When this happens, an expandable panel appears in the chat showing what it searched for and what it found.

#### Ways to Interact

- Type a question or message
- Use the microphone button to speak directly into the chat
- Tap one of the quick-prompt chips:
  - *"How's my week looking?"*
  - *"What should I focus on today?"*
  - *"Am I on track for my targets?"*
  - *"Analyze my logging patterns"*

**Why this matters:** The coach removes the highest-friction part of coaching — constantly re-explaining your situation. It already knows your history, your goals, your tendencies, and your preferred communication style. Every conversation picks up exactly where the last one left off.

### 4.8 Smart Recall — Turning Sessions into Knowledge

Smart Recall transforms logged sessions into a **spaced repetition system** — the same learning method used by medical students, language learners, and elite performers. The difference is that the cards are generated automatically from the user's own work.

#### Getting Started

The feature unlocks after 3 sessions have been logged. A progress bar on the Overview Bonus Card shows how close the user is.

#### How Cards Are Created

After every session save, DiscipLog automatically generates a recall card from that session. Each card contains:

- **Title** — a memorable name for the concept or lesson
- **Prompt** — a challenge question that tests whether the user remembers what they learned
- **Answer** — the correct answer or key insight
- **Why** — the broader reason this lesson matters
- **Rarity** — Spark (surface-level), Forge (deeper), or Boss (critical insight)

Cards build up into a queue. They're never regenerated daily — once created, they persist.

#### The Queue at a Glance

Cards live in one of three states:

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Ready | Due now — the user should review it |
| Coming Back | Snoozed — will reappear in 1 hour |
| Cleared | Completed — retired from the queue |

The Overview Bonus Card reflects the overall state of the queue at all times.

#### Going Through a Recall Session

1. The card opens showing the **title and why**, plus the **recall prompt** prominently
2. The answer area shows a placeholder: *"Try to recall it before you open the answer."*
3. The user thinks through their answer, then clicks **Reveal Answer**
4. The full answer appears, formatted and readable
5. Two buttons:
   - **Got It** — the lesson is solid. Card is permanently retired.
   - **Need Again** — not quite there yet. Card returns in 1 hour.
   - **Ask AI** — opens a scoped conversation about this specific topic (more below)

#### Ask AI on a Card

Each recall card has an **Ask AI** button that opens the AI Coach in a focused mode — strictly scoped to the topic of that card. The user can ask follow-up questions, request simpler explanations, or probe deeper. If the conversation drifts off-topic, the AI politely redirects to the full coach.

#### First-Time Tutorial

New users see a step-by-step guided walkthrough overlaid on top of their first recall card, explaining each zone — what the prompt means, how to use the answer check, and when to press Got It vs. Need Again. The tutorial matches the current visual skin of the app.

#### Automatic Surfacing

After every sprint or manual log save, DiscipLog checks whether there are due cards available and can automatically surface the recall session — making it a natural extension of the session rather than a separate destination.

**Why this matters:** Most productivity apps collect sessions and do nothing with them. Smart Recall makes every session work twice — once when it's logged, and again when the insight is tested and reinforced weeks later. The result is compounding knowledge rather than a growing archive that's never revisited.

### 4.9 Momentum & World Tiers — The Game Beneath the Grind

DiscipLog's gamification layer is not cosmetic. It is a **real-time behavioral feedback system** that makes the interface literally reflect how hard the user has been working.

#### Two Axes

**Daily Energy** measures today's output against daily targets — on a scale from Cold (nothing logged yet) to Peak (all targets hit).

| Level | Name | When |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Cold | No sessions today |
| 1 | Spark | Something logged |
| 2 | Warming | 25%+ of daily targets hit |
| 3 | Burning | 50%+ |
| 4 | Blazing | 75%+ |
| 5 | Peak | 100% — everything done |

**Streak Power** measures long-term consistency — how many consecutive days the user has shown up.

| Level | World | Days |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Basecamp | Starting out |
| 1 | Ember Camp | 1–2 days |
| 2 | Iron Forge | 3–5 days |
| 3 | Sky Citadel | 6–10 days |
| 4 | Obsidian Sanctum | 11+ days |

#### World Skins

Each streak tier applies a distinct visual skin to the entire app — not just a color accent, but a complete atmospheric transformation:

| World | Visual Character |
|---|---|
| Basecamp | Flat, minimal black. A blank slate. |
| Ember Camp | Warm charcoal glow, amber warmth |
| Iron Forge | Rich amber environment, heavy and industrial |
| Sky Citadel | Cool silver-blue, elevated and clean |
| Obsidian Sanctum | Gold-material surfaces, near-white, masterful |

Background gradients shift slowly over more than a second — the app doesn't snap between worlds, it *transitions*.

#### Micro-Interactions That Unlock Over Time

As the streak and daily energy grow, new interactive behaviors unlock progressively — cards that lift and glow on hover, entrance animations that slide or float rather than abruptly appear, shimmer effects on progress bars when targets are hit, confetti on a perfect day, subtle ambient sounds at later tiers, and parallax background layers at the highest levels.

None of these are available on day one. They're earned through consistency.

**Why this matters:** Most gamified apps hand out a badge and move on. DiscipLog makes the *entire interface* feel different at Day 21 compared to Day 1. The app looks and sounds like a reward for showing up — which creates an intrinsic reason to protect the streak that no external reward could manufacture.

### 4.10 Weekly Debrief — The Cinematic Review

Every Sunday, DiscipLog computes the full week's performance and generates an **AI-written narrative review** that appears automatically the next time the user opens the app.

#### What Gets Measured

- Total hours logged across all categories
- Best single day of the week
- Consistency — what percentage of the 7 days had at least one session
- Per-category breakdown compared to the previous week
- The MVP category (highest contribution)
- Streak days maintained

#### What the AI Writes

The AI doesn't just present numbers — it narrates them:

- **A dramatic week title** — something like *"The Week the Grind Got Real"* or *"Steady Hands in a Noisy Week"*
- **A coach note** — a full paragraph written in the user's chosen coach persona, interpreting the week's data with specific callouts
- **MVP category** — a highlighted badge for the top-performing area
- **Hardest day** — a sentence acknowledging where things got difficult
- **Next week's challenge** — one specific, actionable micro-goal to carry forward

#### How It Appears

The debrief is a full-screen overlay with a blurred, darkened backdrop. Each section animates in sequentially — date range, dramatic title, stat grid, coach note, category breakdown with trend arrows, challenge card, and finally a glowing **"Acknowledge & Start New Week"** button.

Past debriefs don't disappear. They're stored in the **Archive** tab — a browseable history of every week.

**Why this matters:** Numbers on a dashboard are information. A narrative is *meaning*. The weekly debrief is the moment the app steps back and says — here's what this week was, here's what it means, and here's what to do next. Users who read their debrief are significantly more likely to act on it than users who scroll a dashboard.

### 4.11 Commitment Tracker — Public Accountability

The Commitment Tracker sits on the Overview dashboard as a weekly goal widget.

#### Setting a Commitment

The user writes a commitment at the start of the week — anything from *"Ship the landing page"* to *"Log gym 5 times"* to *"Read for 30 minutes every day"*. It gets tied to the current week and tracked with a simple Complete / Miss toggle.

#### When a Commitment Is Missed

Marking a commitment as missed doesn't just record a failure — it opens an accountability prompt:

1. A text field appears: *"Why was this missed?"*
2. The user types an honest reason
3. The AI rewrites it into an accountable, forward-facing statement

**Example:**
- What was typed: *"I was tired and just kept putting it off"*
- What the AI produces: *"I let fatigue erode my follow-through this week. Next time I'll break this into a smaller daily commitment so it doesn't depend on having a single high-energy window."*

The rewritten statement is shown in the UI — visible every time the user looks at that week's history.

**Why this matters:** Excuses become invisible when they're only thought. Making the user write them down — and then watch the AI turn them into something accountable — creates a friction that builds self-awareness without shaming. It's the closest thing in the app to a real coach holding you responsible.

### 4.12 Proactive Push Nudges — The App That Pushes Back

DiscipLog doesn't wait to be opened. It pays attention to when you usually show up — and reaches out when you haven't.

#### How the Timing Works

The app tracks the user's historical logging patterns — what time of day sessions typically get logged, and whether that varies by day of the week. When the user hasn't logged today and their usual window is approaching (or passing), the app sends a push notification.

#### The Nudge Tiers

For users with an established logging pattern:

| Tier | Timing | Message intent |
|---|---|---|
| Warmup | ~1 hour before usual time | A gentle "getting close" signal |
| Core | At the usual logging time | "Now's your time" |
| Last Call | 2+ hours past the usual time | "You're running late — don't skip today" |

For newer users still building the habit:

| Tier | Timing | Message intent |
|---|---|---|
| Early Spark | Morning | Encouragement to create the pattern |
| Evening Check | Evening | One last nudge before the day ends |

#### What the Nudge Says

Each notification message is **written fresh by the AI in the user's coach persona** — not a canned template. A Drill Sergeant nudge sounds completely different from a Hype Man nudge. Both are different from a Mentor nudge.

If the user opens the app and has an unread nudge, it appears as a banner at the top of the dashboard — with the message and a quick-start button.

**Why this matters:** Generic daily reminders train users to ignore them within a week. A nudge that arrives at *your* usual time, written in *your* coach's voice, referring to *your* recent pattern — is a fundamentally different interaction. It feels like a check-in from someone who actually knows you.

## Summary

DiscipLog is built on a single conviction: **discipline is a skill, not a personality trait.** Like any skill, it compounds when practiced deliberately and reflected on honestly.

Every feature in the app serves one of three core loops:

1. **Capture** — make logging so fast and natural that it becomes a genuine daily habit
2. **Reflect** — surface patterns, coach conversations, weekly reviews, and recall sessions that let the user learn from their own data
3. **Persist** — world tiers, nudges, commitments, and streak mechanics that make showing up tomorrow feel inevitable

The result is a system that grows with you, coaches you in your preferred style, and makes every logged session count twice — once when it's captured, and again when Smart Recall brings it back.
